Important Update About PixL & Fortnite

Over the past few months, many of you have been confused about why Fortnite updates stopped appearing on PixL. Some thought the app was abandoned, some thought it was a bug, and others weren’t sure what was going on at all. This post is meant to finally clear everything up.

In June 2025, Epic Games introduced new platform-level restrictions that prevented PixL's version of Fortnite from connecting to online matches. Because PixL relied on a custom distribution method to make Fortnite compatible with VoiD FX, this change made PixL’s Fortnite version unusable.

In simple terms: the way PixL worked with Fortnite was blocked by Epic’s new rules.

This was not a bug, not a temporary issue, and not something that could be fixed with an update. It was a permanent, platform-level change.

What Does This Mean?

Due to these restrictions:

  • PixL can no longer safely provide Fortnite builds
  • VoiD FX can no longer work with Fortnite the way it originally did
  • Users attempting to use non-official versions may experience kicks, errors, or restrictions
  • Continuing this system would risk user accounts and device security

Because of this, Fortnite support inside PixL had to be stopped.

This decision was made to protect users and avoid potential problems with accounts, bans, or unexpected behavior.

Was PixL Abandoned?

No. PixL was not abandoned.

What happened was an external platform change that removed a core part of how PixL functioned. Once that happened, continuing in the same direction was no longer viable.

Rather than pushing unsafe or broken updates, everything was paused while a new direction was planned.

The Future of PixL

Instead of trying to fight these restrictions, PixL will be evolving into something better and more sustainable:

A dedicated app store for our mods, tools, and future projects.

Thank You

If you’ve been using PixL from the beginning, thank you for sticking around. Your support genuinely matters, and this change is being made to ensure PixL can continue to exist, not disappear.

More details about the new version of PixL will be shared soon.
